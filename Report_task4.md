## Poor Digital Communication Case



I know a business owner who got an email that looked like it was from a supplier they trusted. The email said they needed to update a payment away. 
The owner did not check if the email was real and they just did what the email said. They sent the payment without calling the supplier to make sure it was really them. 
On they found out that the email was fake and the money went to a bad persons account. This was very bad for the business. It lost a lot of money.

The problem happened because the business was not good at talking to people. They did not make sure things were real. They just believed what the email said.
This would not have happened if they had called the supplier to check or if they had looked carefully at the email address to make sure it was real. They should have used a way to talk to the supplier. 
If they had done these things and taught their employees to be careful this bad thing would probably not have happened. The business would have been safe from this kind of fraud if they had just been more
careful with Digital Communication, like Digital Communication practices and Digital Communication rules.
