## Case Study: UPI Fraud

Unified Payments Interface (UPI) fraud is a rapidly growing cybercrime in which attackers trick users into authorizing fraudulent transactions. Unlike traditional hacking, it relies heavily on social engineering
rather than technical breaches.

The fraud typically begins when a scammer contacts the victim via phone, SMS, or messaging apps such as WhatsApp, posing as a bank official, customer support agent, or buyer. Step one involves building trust by creating urgency
or offering attractive deals. In step two, the fraudster asks the victim to “receive” money by approving a collect request or scanning a QR code. Step three involves misleading instructions, 
such as entering a UPI PIN under the pretense of verification. Once the victim enters the PIN, the money is instantly transferred from their account. In some cases, scammers also send fake links that mimic official 
apps like Google Pay or PhonePe to steal login credentials.

The most common targets include elderly individuals, new digital payment users, small business owners, and people in urgent financial situations. Limited awareness and high trust in digital platforms make these 
groups especially vulnerable.

The consequences can be severe. Victims often face immediate financial loss, and recovery is difficult due to the real-time nature of UPI transactions. Additionally, there is emotional stress, 
loss of trust in digital systems, and potential misuse of personal data for further fraud. This highlights the importance of awareness, caution, and digital literacy in preventing such crimes.
